# Before/After Example: Iteration in Action

This is a sanitized example showing how the refinement loop improves a generated donor outreach email across iterations. Names and identifying details have been replaced with placeholders.

## Context

- **Donor:** A regional business owner with a prior small donation to NLEats two years ago
- **LinkedIn-sourced context:** Recently posted about expanding their business to a second location and hiring locally
- **Campaign goal:** Re-engage past donors for NLEats' ongoing food distribution program

## Iteration 1 (Generated)

> Subject: Support NLEats' Mission
>
> Hi there,
>
> I hope this email finds you well! NLEats is a community organization dedicated to fighting food insecurity through donation and distribution programs. We rely on the generosity of supporters like you to continue our important work.
>
> Would you consider making a donation to support our ongoing efforts? Every contribution makes a difference.
>
> Thank you for your continued support!

### Evaluation Scores (1-10)
| Dimension | Score | Feedback |
|---|---|---|
| Persuasion | 5/10 | Generic mission statement, no specific connection to this donor |
| Clarity | 7/10 | Clear ask, but unremarkable |
| Personalization | 3/10 | Doesn't use prior donation history or LinkedIn context at all |
| Relevance | 2/10 | No connection to the donor's recent business expansion |

**Overall: FAIL** (Personalization and Relevance below 7)

## Iteration 2 (After Refinement)

> Subject: Growing Together — An Update from NLEats
>
> Hi there,
>
> Congratulations on the new location — growth like that takes real commitment, and it's exciting to see local businesses expanding and hiring in the community.
>
> A couple of years ago, you supported NLEats' food distribution work, and that support helped us reach families who needed it most. As our program continues to grow alongside businesses like yours, we'd love to have you with us again — even a small contribution helps us plan for the families we'll serve this season.
>
> Would you be open to continuing that support? Happy to share what your past contribution helped make possible.

### Evaluation Scores (1-10)
| Dimension | Score | Feedback |
|---|---|---|
| Persuasion | 8/10 | Connects donor's own growth to NLEats' growth — relatable framing |
| Clarity | 9/10 | Single clear ask, easy to follow |
| Personalization | 8/10 | References both prior donation history and recent business news |
| Relevance | 9/10 | Directly tied to the donor's recent LinkedIn post about expansion |

**Overall: PASS** — Sent

## What Changed

Iteration 1 used generic mission language that **could have been sent to any donor with any history**. The evaluation feedback flagged that neither the donor's prior contribution nor their recent LinkedIn activity were used at all.

Iteration 2 used both pieces of context simultaneously — the donor's own business growth became the entry point, and their prior donation became the reason to ask again. This single change improved Personalization, Relevance, *and* Persuasion together, because the donor's recent growth and NLEats' need for continued support are framed as parallel stories.

This is a common pattern: **a single well-chosen piece of context, used as the framing device for the entire email, often improves multiple dimensions at once** — compared to making isolated edits to address each dimension separately.
